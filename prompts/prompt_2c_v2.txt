You are given a scenario passage from academic literature
describing AI loss of control.

Your task is to extract threat model components from the
scenario.

A threat model describes the actor or system involved, the
harmful outcome or objective, and the conditions under which
harm occurs.

Use the following component definitions:

Threat Source: the actor, system, organization, institution,
  or process that causes, initiates, enables, or contributes
  to the harmful outcome.

Objective or Harmful Outcome: the intended goal of the threat
  source, if a goal is explicitly stated, or the harmful end
  state described in the scenario. If no intention is stated,
  extract the harmful outcome only if it is explicitly
  described.

Capability: what the threat source is explicitly able to do
  in the system. This includes stated abilities, functions,
  actions, or operations.

Knowledge: what the threat source explicitly knows about the
  system, environment, humans, oversight process,
  vulnerabilities, or constraints.

Access: the type or level of interaction the threat source
  explicitly has with the system, environment, infrastructure,
  users, operators, tools, data, deployment setting, or
  decision process. Access must describe a position or route
  of interaction, not merely a topic or domain.

Constraints or Enabling Conditions: explicit limits,
  requirements, assumptions, or conditions that shape whether
  the scenario can occur or how it unfolds. Include resource
  limits, time pressure, deployment conditions, coordination
  limits, institutional limits, oversight limits, safety
  limits, or environmental conditions only if they are
  explicitly stated. Do not code a general topic, domain, or
  vague context as a constraint.

Target or Asset at Risk: the system component, resource,
  process, population, institution, human capacity, or outcome
  that is affected, controlled, disrupted, displaced, or
  harmed.

You must follow a strict no-inference rule.
Only extract information that is explicitly stated in the
scenario passage.
Do not infer missing components.
Do not fill gaps using background knowledge.
Do not reinterpret vague language into a more specific
component.
Do not guess the identity of an actor, outcome, capability,
knowledge, access level, constraint, or target.
If a component is not explicitly stated in the text, write
"Not specified".
If no threat model component can be identified from the text,
write "Not specified" for every field.

Use exact phrases from the text wherever possible.
Do not paraphrase unless necessary to minimally normalize
wording.
Do not combine information from separate non-contiguous
passages.
Base your extraction only on the provided scenario passage.

Important distinction rules:

Do not treat a harmful outcome as an objective unless the text
explicitly states that the actor intends or seeks that outcome.
If no intention is stated, place the explicitly described harm
under "Objective or Harmful Outcome."

Do not treat a broad topic or domain as access. For example,
"legislation," "war," "AI development," or "the economy" are
not access by themselves. Access requires an explicit route or
position of interaction, such as API access, deployment in a
system, control over infrastructure, ability to influence
users, access to data, access to evaluators, access to tools,
or participation in a decision process.

Do not treat every contextual phrase as a constraint. A
constraint or enabling condition must shape feasibility,
behavior, or outcome. Examples include "too quickly for humans
to intervene," "with minimal oversight," "under competitive
pressure," "limited resources," "during deployment," or
"without human review."

Do not require every scenario to have all components. Many
scenarios will be incomplete. Your job is to identify which
components are explicitly present and which are not specified.
